About m-CAFEs

Toward a Predictive Science of Soil Microbiomes

m-CAFEs is a multi-institutional Science Focus Area studying how microbes around plant roots assemble, interact, and function. Our team combines next-generation biotechnologies — including CRISPR-Cas and bacteriophage tools — with AI/ML models and unique laboratory ecosystems to understand soil microbial communities at unprecedented resolution.

By learning how genes and metabolites direct community assembly, we are building the foundation for a new kind of predictive microbiome science — one that can support sustainable bioenergy crops like sorghum, advance the DOE Genesis Mission, and help address real-world challenges in agriculture and the environment.

DOE BER Alignment: AI is a primary focus of the DOE Genesis Mission, with the potential to greatly accelerate scientific discovery. m-CAFEs combines AI/ML with biotechnology innovation to enable predictive microbiome science in support of DOE-BER missions.

Our Science

Four Integrated Research Thrusts

m-CAFEs research advances along four connected thrusts, each contributing to a unified goal: to understand, predict, design, and support microbial communities that matter for DOE missions.

Thrust 01

Developing New Biotechnologies

We build next-generation tools — CRISPR-Cas systems and phage-based approaches — to study how individual genes and microbes shape soil community function in their natural environment.

Primary PIsDoudna, Barrangou, Banfield, Deutschbauer, Mukhopadhyay
Thrust 02

Understanding Microbial Community Assembly

Using fabricated ecosystems and multi-omics, we resolve how microbes interact, exchange metabolites, and assemble into stable communities around plant roots.

Primary PIsNorthen, Zhalnina, Chakraborty, Banfield, Mukhopadhyay
Thrust 03 Genesis Mission

AI/ML Modeling

We develop AI/ML models that learn from automated experiments and predict how microbial communities behave — a core capability for the DOE Genesis Mission and a foundation for predictive microbiome science.

Primary PIsSegrè, Wang, Hill-Maini, Northen
Thrust 04

Rhizosphere Engineering for Sorghum

We apply these tools to sorghum, a key bioenergy crop, to understand and support how root microbiomes contribute to plant productivity in real-world conditions.

Primary PIsEudes, Zhalnina, Northen, Chakraborty
